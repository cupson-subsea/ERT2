Key lithological formations encountered during near-surface and engineering evaluations

Igneous (Extrusive)

Basalt

Fine-grained, dark volcanic rock rich in iron and magnesium. Composes most of the oceanic crust framework.

Engineering Use: High-strength aggregate for subsea pipeline armoring, concrete reinforcement, and road foundations.
Igneous (Intrusive)

Granite

Coarse-grained plutonic rock rich in quartz and feldspar. Forms the deep, unyielding structural basement blocks.

Engineering Use: Heavy-duty structural columns, breakwater riprap blocks to prevent coastal erosion.
Sedimentary (Clastic)

Sandstone

Compacted silicate sand grains. Possesses high porosity and permeability channels across sedimentary basins.

Engineering Use: Ideal reservoir matrix for groundwater aquiferous systems and petroleum trap storage systems.
Sedimentary (Fissile)

Shale

Fine-grained rock consolidated from mud and clay minerals. Highly impermeable layer exhibiting low electrical resistivity.

Engineering Use: Functions as the perfect source rock and sealing cap layer to prevent fluid migration.
